Someone just filled in your contact form. They're interested. They're warm. And right now — in the next 5 minutes — they're deciding whether to also fill in the form on your competitor's website.

The business that responds first wins the conversation. Not necessarily the contract, but the conversation. And the conversation is where trust starts.

The problem? You're in a meeting. You're focused. You didn't see the notification. And by the time you get back to them, it's been four hours.

This post shows you how to fix that — without gluing yourself to your inbox.

## Why Speed in Lead Response Actually Matters

Research from Harvard Business Review found that businesses that contacted leads within one hour were **7x more likely** to have a meaningful conversation than those who waited just two hours. Seven times.

This isn't about being pushy. It's about being present at the moment someone is most interested.

Automated follow-up doesn't replace the personal conversation — it *ensures* it happens. You respond instantly (even while you're in a meeting), buy yourself time, and signal from the very first interaction that you're organised and reliable.

## How It Works: Step by Step

### Step 1: Set Up a Consistent Lead Capture Form

For this automation to work, your leads need to come through a single, consistent channel. If enquiries are currently arriving via Instagram DMs, email, LinkedIn messages, WhatsApp, and your contact form all at once — start by pointing people to one place: a form.

Use **Tally** (free) or Typeform to build a clean contact form. At minimum, capture:

- First name
- Email address
- Company / organisation
- What they're looking for
- How they heard about you

> 📸 *Screenshot: Tally contact form showing the five fields above, with a clean, simple layout*

> 💡 **Pro tip:** Add a "How urgent is this?" field with options like "Just exploring," "Within the next month," and "ASAP." This helps you prioritise follow-up without any manual sorting.

> ✅ **After this step:** Every lead comes through one channel, with consistent data you can act on.

---

### Step 2: Create Your Lead Tracker

Before you build the automation, create a simple lead tracker where all submissions will be stored. Use **Airtable** (recommended) or a Google Sheet.

Your tracker should have these columns:

| Column | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Name | From form |
| Email | From form |
| Company | From form |
| Enquiry | What they're looking for |
| Source | How they heard about you |
| Date Received | Auto-populated by Make.com |
| Urgency | From the form (if you added that field) |
| Response Sent | Yes / No — update this manually after you reply |
| Follow-Up Sent | Yes / No — auto-updated by Make.com |
| Status | New / In Progress / Won / Lost |
| Notes | Free text — your personal notes |

> 📸 *Screenshot: Airtable lead tracker base showing the columns above with a few sample rows*

> 💡 **Pro tip:** Add a "Source" column and keep it updated. Over time, you'll see exactly which channels bring in the leads most likely to convert — that's valuable data that usually goes uncollected.

> ✅ **After this step:** You have a single source of truth for every lead that comes in.

### Step 3: Build the Make.com Scenario

Your Make.com scenario fires every time someone submits your Tally form. Here's what it needs to do:

**Module 1 — Tally: Watch for New Submissions**
This is your trigger. Make.com monitors your Tally form and fires the scenario every time a new response comes in.

**Module 2 — Airtable: Create a Record**
Immediately adds the lead's details to your tracker. Map each form field to the corresponding Airtable column. Also set the "Date Received" to today's date and "Response Sent" to "No."

**Module 3 — Gmail: Send Acknowledgement Email**
Sends the acknowledgement email to the prospect within seconds of their form submission. More on writing this in Step 4.

**Module 4 — Slack: Send Notification**
Posts a message to your #leads Slack channel (or sends a direct message to you) with:
- Lead's name and company
- What they're looking for
- The enquiry urgency level
- A link to the Airtable record

> ✅ **After this step:** Every new lead is tracked, acknowledged, and brought to your attention automatically — whether you're at your desk or not.

### Step 4: Write the Acknowledgement Email

This email goes out within seconds of the form submission. It's often the first piece of writing a lead sees from you — which means it's doing real relationship work even though it's automated.

**What it needs to do:**
- Confirm their message was received (removes anxiety on their end)
- Set a clear expectation for when they'll hear from you personally
- Give them something useful in the meantime — a relevant resource, your most useful blog post, or a link to learn more about your work
- Feel warm and personal, not automated

Here's a template that works:

> *Hi &#123;&#123;first_name&#125;&#125;,*
>
> *Thanks for getting in touch — I've received your message about &#123;&#123;enquiry_topic&#125;&#125;.*
>
> *I'll be back in touch within &#123;&#123;response_timeframe&#125;&#125; to talk through how I can help. In the meantime, you might find this useful: &#123;&#123;relevant_link&#125;&#125;.*
>
> *Talk soon,*
> *&#123;&#123;your_name&#125;&#125;*

Keep it short. The goal is not to sell — it's to confirm you're real, you're responsive, and you'll be in touch.

> 📸 *Screenshot: Gmail module in Make.com showing the acknowledgement email template with variables highlighted*

> ⚠️ **Read this email out loud before saving it.** If it sounds like a bot wrote it, rewrite it. The test is simple: would you send this exact message to a friend who asked for a recommendation?

### Step 5: Add the 24-Hour Follow-Up

If you haven't personally responded within 24 hours, Make.com sends a second email automatically. This is your safety net — the lead doesn't go cold just because you got busy.

The email is short and direct:

> *Hi &#123;&#123;first_name&#125;&#125;,*
>
> *Just following up on your enquiry — I'd love to connect and hear more about what you're working on.*
>
> *Here's a link to book a quick call at a time that works for you: &#123;&#123;calendly_link&#125;&#125;*
>
> *&#123;&#123;your_name&#125;&#125;*

**How to build the 24-hour delay in Make.com:**

Set up a separate, scheduled scenario that runs every morning. It checks your Airtable tracker for any leads where:
- `Response Sent = No`
- `Date Received < 24 hours ago`
- `Follow-Up Sent = No`

If it finds any, it sends the follow-up email and updates the "Follow-Up Sent" field to "Yes."

> 📸 *Screenshot: Make.com scheduled scenario with Airtable filter conditions and Gmail follow-up module*

> 💡 **Pro tip:** Once you send the follow-up, update the "Follow-Up Sent" field in Airtable. This prevents the scenario from sending multiple follow-ups to the same lead.

### Step 6: Tag and Categorise Leads Automatically

As your tracker grows, it becomes hard to prioritise without good categorisation. Use Make.com to automatically tag leads based on what they enquire about:

- If the enquiry mentions "social media" → tag as `Content`
- If it mentions "website" → tag as `Web Development`
- If it mentions "automation" or "Make.com" → tag as `Automation`

You can do basic keyword matching in Make.com using the "Text parser" module to search for keywords in the enquiry field.

> 📸 *Screenshot: Make.com Text Parser module checking for keywords in the enquiry field and setting an Airtable tag*

> ✅ **After this step:** Your lead tracker is organised, searchable, and tells you at a glance where each lead came from and what they want.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Making the acknowledgement email sound automated.**
The whole point of this email is to feel human. Clients should think you wrote it yourself. If there's any doubt, rewrite it. Test it by sending it to a friend and asking if it sounds like you.

**Not having a clear call to action in the follow-up email.**
Every follow-up email should have one thing the prospect can do — book a call, reply with a question, read a resource. Don't leave them with a message and nothing to click.

**Letting leads pile up in the tracker without updating statuses.**
The tracker is only useful if you keep it updated. Build a 5-minute habit at the end of every workday to update the status of each lead you've been in contact with. The automation gets them in; you keep them moving.

**Sending to all platforms instead of one form.**
If you don't consolidate your lead capture into one form, the automation can't fire. The first step is always directing enquiries to a single, consistent channel.

**Setting the 24-hour follow-up to repeat.**
Make sure the follow-up only sends once per lead. If the scenario doesn't check for "Follow-Up Sent = Yes," it'll send the same email every day. That's not a nudge — that's spam.
